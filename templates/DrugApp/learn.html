Introduction to the Opioid Epidemic

Opioids are a class of very strong pain relievers. They include medications such as acetaminophen/oxycodone (Percocet), morphine (Kadian), and oxycodone (OxyContin).Doctors typically prescribe opioids to relieve pain after surgery or an injury. In 2019, doctors in the United States wrote more than 153 million prescriptionsTrusted Source for these medications. While these medications are very effective pain relievers, people who take them are also prone to misuse and the development of physical and psychological dependence.

Between 8.4 and 11.6 percent of people who used opioids for 1 to 30 days developed depression within the 12 months following their opioid use. The longer they used opioids, the greater their risk of developing depression.

While opioids are effective at relieving pain, they can lead to physical dependence and addiction. Dependence means you need opioids to function well. Addiction is when you continue to use opioids even though they cause harmful effects. Opioids are believed to change brain chemistry in a way that makes you need more and more of them to get the same effect. Over time, taking increasingly larger doses leads to dependence.

This information was quoted from Healthline, go check out their site for more about opioids and mental health.

U.S. Opioid Prescriptions

The opioid crisis is devastating communities across America. The latest statistics indicate that the total yearly overdose deaths due to opioids reached a new high of 70,237 in 2017. In October 2017, President Trump declared the opioid crisis a public health emergency.

Currently no system exists that allows government officials and prescribers to simply view the volume of prescriptions that are taking place overall. So we have created this site to help adress that problem. We have a database of information about prescribers and drugs. The data are sorted and accessable so that all parties can freely analyze the data themselves and seek understanding. Our hope is that through this understanding we can all work to make the world a better place!

We also have taken the time to provide links to other learning material to help everyone better understand addiction, recovery, and how they can best help those around them.
